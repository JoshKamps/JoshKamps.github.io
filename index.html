Bio

I am currently a first-year PhD student in the Department of Security and Crime Science at UCL. My research interests lie at the intersection of the computational and social sciences, concentrating on emerging crime types from a computational perspective. My current research is regarding cryptocurrency fraud, with with a focus on market manipulation and the social forces behind it.

BSc Computer Science @ VU Amsterdam
MSc Crime Science @ UCL

Research

My focus has thus far been on crytpocurrency pump-and-dump schemes: I have investigated ways to computationally detect them, the chatrooms where the perpetrators organise themselves, and which coins have a high potential for this type of fraud.

As the first step of my PhD I am working on mapping out the landscape of cryptocurrency fraud more broadly. This involves researching both new crimes in the sector (crypto-dependent), as well as many old crimes which have been given a new life in the context of cryptocurrencies (crypto-enabled).
